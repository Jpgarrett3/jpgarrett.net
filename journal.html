Appalachian Fantasy Project

This is an ongoing project exploring Appalachian settings, folklore, and history through the lens of fantasy. The goal is not romanticization, but respect for place, labor, and memory.

Guiding Ideas

  • Fantasy rooted in real geography and material conditions
  • Folklore as lived practice, not aesthetic flavor
  • Power, extraction, and survival
  • Magic that costs something

Notes & Fragments

The mountains remember. Not in a mystical sense, but through scars: strip mines, abandoned towns, altered rivers. Any magic here should operate the same way.

Selected Influences

  • Appalachian folklore and oral histories
  • Southern Gothic fiction
  • Weird and ecological fantasy
